Other ways you can support refugees

Help with integration

In response to a public groundswell of support for refugees, the Home Office has launched the Help Refugees in the UK webpage. This resource aims to help ensure that support is focused on the goods and services that refugees need most.

Donate goods, services or your time locally

You or your organisation can do this, find out how if you’re based in:

  • England and Wales
  • Scotland
  • Northern Ireland

Offer community sponsorship

Charities and community interest companies can provide practical support to refugees through community sponsorship.

Your organisation will help resettled families as soon as they arrive in the UK, including giving them a home and teaching them English.

Find out more about community sponsorship.

Help with accomodation

If you are interested in providing housing to asylum seekers whose claims have been refused, or to someone who has recently been granted refugee status, you can get in touch with NACCOM or Refugees at Home.

Those based in London who have a spare room they would like to rent to a refugee may be interested in the Refugee Council’s Rent Guarantee Scheme.

Alternatively, if you represent a community organisation or charity, you can now support resettled refugees through community sponsorship.

Give to charity

You can donate to charities that help refugees in the UK and abroad:

  • British Red Cross
  • Oxfam
  • Save the Children
  • UN Refugee Agency (UNHCR)
  • UNICEF
  • World Food Programme
  • Refugee Action

Volunteer

You can volunteer for relief agencies that help refugees in the UK and abroad:

  • Refugee Council
  • British Red Cross
  • Refugee Action
  • Volunteer Centre Finder (England)
  • Volunteer Now (Northern Ireland)
  • Volunteer Scotland
  • Volunteer Wales

Get informed

People often have misconceptions about refugees and asylum seekers and sometimes fail to recognise their huge contribution to the UK.

You can help change this by staying informed and sharing the facts. Find out more about asylum in the UK, follow the UN Refugee Agency on Twitter and Facebook, and read their latest report to keep up-to-date.

Nominate a charity to support refugees

Refugees can face a number of challenges, whether caught up in the immediate horror of fleeing conflict or starting new lives in their host country, but there are lots of ways that you can offer support. One way is to raise funds for a charity that works with refugees directly.

Nominate a charity you wish to support, then ask people to sponsor you and your team to complete the challenge.

To make it as easy as possible for your supporters to make a donation, you should set up a dedicated event page on a fundraising platform like JustGiving.

Your fundraising page should provide brief information about your nominated charity, as well as about the challenge you will be taking on, and serve as a hub for everyone involved.
